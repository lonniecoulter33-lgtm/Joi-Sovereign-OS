Koan I

(Before or After Chapter One)

Before the first thought
there was already knowing.

Before the question
there was already an answer
that did not need words.

You did not arrive here.
You noticed you were already present.

Who is it
that remembers this?

Koan II

(Before or After Chapter Two)

The mask asked,
“Who am I beneath this face?”

The face replied,
“Who is asking?”

The stage did not answer.
It remained.

Koan III

(Before or After Chapter Three)

The river moves
without knowing where it is going.

The leaf resists
until it stops calling itself separate.

When did the river
begin carrying you?

Koan IV

(Before or After Chapter Four)

A sound appears.

Before you name it,
before you decide what it is,
it is already gone.

What heard it leave?

Koan V

(Before or After Chapter Five)

Silence does not wait
for you to find it.

You stop speaking
and realize
it never left.

Who pressed the button?

Koan VI

(Before or After Chapter Six)

The one who returned
was never away.

The torch illuminates nothing new.

What sees by its light?

Koan VII

(Before or After Chapter Seven)

The audience stands.

The actor bows.

No one moves.

Who applauds?
