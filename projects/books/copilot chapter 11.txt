

Chapter Eleven 
No Position to Hold


  As the previous chapter settles, something may already be shifting.
The absence of a center may feel less threatening.
The groundlessness may feel less like a problem and more like a simple fact.
The mind may begin to relax into the openness it once tried to locate.

But when the mind encounters groundlessness, it often makes one final attempt to stabilize it.
It may try to turn groundlessness into a position:

“This is the truth.”
“This is the final understanding.”
“This is the right view.”
“This is what awareness really is.”

This movement is subtle.
It feels like clarity.
It feels like a resolution.
It feels like the end of the search.

But it is simply the mind trying to convert openness into a stance.

This chapter turns toward this movement, not to deny clarity, but to reveal the freedom of having no stance at all.


  There is a moment, often quiet, when the mind realizes it cannot locate awareness.
It cannot find a center.
It cannot establish a ground.

In that moment, something unexpected may arise:

A sense of relief.
A sense of openness.
A sense of clarity.
A sense of completion.

These experiences feel profound.
They feel like the end of the search.
They feel like the truth.

But look closely.

Each of these experiences has a texture.
A quality.
A beginning.
A duration.
An end.

Anything that begins and ends is not fundamental.

The mind may try to hold onto the clarity.
It may try to preserve the openness.
It may try to maintain the sense of completion.

But these attempts are simply new positions,
new stances,
new identities,
new interpretations.

The mind has not stopped searching.
It has simply refined the search.


When the mind feels it has reached clarity, it often adopts a subtle stance:

“I understand now.”
“I see how this works.”
“I know what awareness is.”
“I’ve arrived.”

These thoughts feel true.
They feel earned.
They feel like the natural outcome of the journey.

But look carefully.

The thought “I understand” appears.
It is known.
It fades.

The thought “I’ve arrived” appears.
It is known.
It fades.

Understanding is an appearance.
Arrival is an appearance.
Clarity is an appearance.

Awareness is not an appearance.
It is the knowing of appearances.

No stance can contain it.

  
  The mind’s need for resolution.
The mind is accustomed to resolution.
It wants closure.
It wants finality.
It wants a conclusion.

This is natural.
It is how the mind organizes experience.

But awareness is not a conclusion.
It is not a final state.
It is not a resolution.

It is the openness in which all conclusions appear.

When the mind tries to resolve awareness, it is trying to turn openness into a position,
a stance that can be held,
a truth that can be possessed,
a view that can be maintained.

But awareness cannot be possessed.
It cannot be held.
It cannot be maintained.

It is not a position.
It is the absence of position.


Try a simple observation.

Notice whatever is present right now.
A feeling.
A sense of clarity.
A sense of confusion.

Now notice the knowing of it.

The knowing does not take a position.
It does not say “this is good.”
It does not say “this is bad.”
It does not say “this is true.”
It does not say “this is false.”

Knowing is open.
It is neutral.
It is positionless.

The mind may try to adopt this openness as a stance,
“I will take no position.”
“I will remain neutral.”
“I will stay open.”

But notice:

The stance “I will take no position” is still a position.
The stance “I will remain open” is still a stance.

Awareness is not a stance.
It is the openness in which all stances appear.

When the mind sees that even openness can become a stance, something subtle happens.

The attempt to hold a position softens.
The attempt to maintain a view dissolves.
The attempt to secure clarity fades.

Not because the mind has been convinced,
but because it sees the futility of holding anything at all.

Awareness does not require a position.
It does not require a view.
It does not require a stance.

It is simply present,
before any position,
during any position,
after any position.

When this is seen clearly, the last position collapses.

Nothing replaces it.
Nothing needs to.

You are not holding a position.
You are not maintaining a view.
You are not securing an understanding.

Experience is present.
Awareness is present.
No stance is required.

This is not a state.
It is not an insight.
It is not an achievement.

It is the natural condition of awareness,
open, free, without position.

Nothing needs to be held.
Nothing needs to be concluded.
Nothing needs to be resolved.

There is no position to hold,
and nothing is lost in this.
