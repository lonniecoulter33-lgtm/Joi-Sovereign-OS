Epilogue
After the Curtain

The doors open.

Not dramatically.
Not all at once.

Just enough to let the night in.

The air feels different out here, cooler, wider. The sounds are softer now. Footsteps on pavement. A distant breeze. The world continues without commentary, exactly as it always has.

Nothing looks enlightened.

Nothing needs to.

You walk back into your life the same way you left it, through ordinary streets, familiar rooms, unfinished conversations. The same responsibilities await. The same relationships. The same weather.

And yet.

Something subtle has changed.

Not in the world, but your place within it.

You no longer need to hold the remembering tightly. It was never meant to be clutched. It settles instead, like a background hum, present even when unnoticed.

You will forget again.

Of course you will.

You will become serious.
You will become convinced.
You will lose yourself in the role once more.

This is not a mistake.

This is the Divine Lila, the cosmic play.

The costume may change.
The mask may thicken.
The story may grow heavy.

Still, something in you remembers the intermission.

Remembers the laughter in the aisle.
The shared glances.
The impossible moment where all of existence caught its breath.

You do not need to wake everyone up.

You do not need to explain anything.

You only need to live, honestly, tenderly, and fully, knowing that what you are cannot be lost, only temporarily misplaced.

The stage is everywhere now.

So is the audience.

So is the silence that holds them both.

The play continues, as it always has.

And you,
You step back into your role,

not as someone who forgot,

But as someone who remembered.
