Reintegration

This chapter does not describe an outcome.

It does not explain what may or may not have occurred.
It does not assume that anything changed.
It does not attempt to preserve anything.

It addresses ordinary functioning.

Thought continues.

Thought appears when it appears.
It stops when it stops.
It resumes without permission.

This is not a problem to solve.
It is how cognition works.

Nothing that matters here depends on reducing thought.
Nothing that matters here depends on sustaining quiet.
Nothing that matters here depends on attention behaving a certain way.

Desire continues.

Preferences arise.
Aversion arises.
Interest arises.

None of this indicates confusion.
None of this indicates clarity.

Desire does not obscure awareness.
Aversion does not obscure awareness.

They appear.
They move.
They pass.

Emotion continues.

Emotion may be strong.
Emotion may be subtle.
Emotion may feel familiar or unfamiliar.

Emotion does not need interpretation.
Emotion does not require distance.
Emotion does not require meaning.

Feeling what is felt is not an achievement.
Avoiding what is felt is not a failure.

Decision-making continues.

Choices are made.
Actions occur.
Consequences follow.

There is no special way this needs to happen.
There is no correct internal posture required.

Responsibility does not disappear.
Care does not disappear.
Attention to circumstances does not disappear.

Functioning remains intact.

Confusion may appear.

Confusion does not indicate regression.
Confusion does not need to be resolved.

Clarity may appear.

Clarity does not indicate arrival.
Clarity does not need to be maintained.

Confusion and clarity alternate.
Neither governs what is present.

There may be an impulse to protect something.

An impulse to keep experience a certain way.
An impulse to avoid losing something subtle.
An impulse to return to a previous moment.

These impulses are understandable.

They are not instructions.

Nothing essential can be protected.
Nothing essential can be lost.

This is not reassurance.
It is observation.

There may also be an impulse to explain.

To interpret what happened.
To frame it as progress.
To compare before and after.

Explanation has practical uses.
It helps communication.
It helps memory.

It does not confirm what matters here.

Explanation adds structure.
It does not add access.

Life continues in ordinary ways.

Work.
Conversation.
Conflict.
Pleasure.
Fatigue.
Routine.

None of this interferes.

There is no need to simplify life.
There is no need to purify experience.
There is no need to align behavior with an idea.

Ordinariness does not contradict anything.

If nothing in this book seems relevant now, that is acceptable.

If something in this book seems relevant later, that is acceptable.

Relevance is not evidence.
Irrelevance is not evidence.

Nothing here depends on remembering these pages.

There is no final instruction.

There is no state to stabilize.
There is no understanding to complete.
There is no position to occupy.

What functions does so without management.

Reading may end here.
Or it may not.

Either way, nothing needs to be carried forward.
