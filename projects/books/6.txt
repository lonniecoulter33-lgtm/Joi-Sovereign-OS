Chapter Six
Returning with the Torch

Nothing dramatic announces the return.

No trumpet sounds.
No sky opens.
No final understanding arrives to seal the moment. You are simply returning without forgetting.

The absence of spectacle is not an omission of the experience; it is the point. What arrives without noise does not require validation. What endures does not need to announce itself.

Awakening is not an escape from the world.
It is a change in how the world is inhabited. How do you now view the dramas of others and of yourself?

After stillness has been touched, after awareness has known itself without reference to body, thought, or sensation, the return can feel strangely quiet. There is no fireworks display announcing re-entry into the world of phenomena, no dramatic shift visible to others. The body resumes breathing. The senses resume reporting. The stage reforms exactly as it was.

Life picks up its rhythm without hesitation, as if nothing had happened at all.

And yet, nothing is the same.

The difference is not visible. It is a loosening so subtle that it can be mistaken for nothing happening.

There is no ceremonial crossing of a threshold. No visible marker separating before from after. The world looks exactly as it did moments ago.
The marketplace remains the marketplace. The body remains the body. Time continues its ordinary pace.

The same sounds echo. The same routines continue. The same responsibilities await.

And yet, something is unmistakably different.

You are no longer searching.

Not because you found an answer, but because the one who was searching has relaxed. The restless movement toward becoming has softened into a simple being. The sense of arrival is quiet, almost ordinary, so ordinary that it feels as if everybody should remember this state of consciousness, the difference being except that you remembered you had forgotten.

Advaita Vedanta calls this recognition sahaja samadhi—not absorption away from life, but truth functioning effortlessly within it.

There is no thrill to cling to. No experience to preserve. No insight to defend. Just an absence of urgency where urgency once lived.
The mind still works, but no longer works for you.

Thoughts arise, but they are no longer commanders. They are tools laid down when not needed. The endless commentary loses its authority. Silence no longer feels empty. It is welcomed and enjoyed as what sustains existence.

And that is the accomplishment.

This is how it has always been.

Nothing new has entered the scene. Nothing essential has been added. The shift is not in reality, but in our perception of it, we are no longer strained, no longer divided.

Kant might say the noumenon was never altered, and only the lens of interpretation fell silent.

Those who remembered before you did not stay apart from the world. They returned to it. The Buddha did not remain beneath the tree. Laozi did not disappear into abstraction. Rumi danced in the marketplaces. Jesus walked among fishermen and laborers.

Enlightenment was never an escape from form, but clarity within it. A return to our primal state.

They did not retreat into a permanent stillness. They did not cling to revelation as a private possession.

They remembered, and then they came back. To share their journey with others so that they might remember that they, too, have only forgotten.

In Buddhist terms, this is the Bodhisattva vow enacted naturally, not as an obligation, but out of compassion for the betterment of all humanity.

Back into noise. Back into movement. Back into human life, with all its messiness and beauty intact.

Not to escape life, but to live it without confusion.

They did not carry doctrines so much as presence. They did not impose truth; they embodied it. Wherever they walked, something subtle was illuminated, not by force, but by resonance.

The light was always there.

In Kabbalistic language, this is referred to as tikkun—repair not by correction, but by alignment.

Their clarity did not confront, it only revealed. It did not persuade. It invited. And so I invite you now.

As they were light bearers on a path that had become dark only because it was unexamined. I invite you to shine your light for others to see the light within themselves. The light in all of us. One candle to light them all.

The darkness was never evil. It was never a fault of man. It was simply unseen.

Now you understand why.

Truth does not remove you from the world. It returns you to it, free of entanglement. You are said to be of the world, but not in it, not withdrawn, not aloof, and no longer submerged.

Transcendental Meditation describes this as action from rest, and activity emerging from stillness rather than effort.

You stand within life without being claimed by it.

Like the lotus flower.

Its roots reach deep into the water, down into the depths of the mud beneath. The lotus then grows up through the water, nourished fully by it. Yet its petals rise above the surface, untouched by the current. The water supports it, but does not claim it. The lotus is untouched by the mud below.

This is non-attachment without avoidance of social interaction.

The lotus does not reject the water, nor does it drown in it. It belongs to both realms without conflict.

So it is with you.

You still live here. You work, love, struggle, and age. You will feel joy and loss. Nothing human has been excluded. But inwardly, you are no longer swept away by the movement of life. The current moves beneath you now.

Mindfulness no longer requires effort, as it has become second nature.

Thoughts still arise. Emotions still pass. Circumstances still change. But they do not define the ground you stand on.

You participate without drowning.

This is the return with the torch.

Not a descent from truth, but its expression. Not a loss of stillness, but its movement from within it.

Wu Wei continues, now expressed through speech, work, and relationships.

And with it comes a quiet responsibility, not an obligation, but a natural consequence. When confusion has fallen away, clarity becomes visible. When stillness is embodied, others feel it too. This isn't because you tell them, but because your presence no longer adds noise. to the chaos of life.

The nervous system entrains. Silence teaches silently.

You become the torchbearer.

The light shines from you.

You are no longer amplifying fear, urgency, or separation. In a world saturated with tension, this absence is felt in everything that you do. This is to embody the way of Zen. Your walking becomes an art expressed as Zen walking. When you speak, you speak only in truth, simple yet devoid of any disillusionment.

This is ethical action without moral strain.

You are carrying the torch now.

Not as a teacher above others, but as a reminder among them. Not as someone who knows more, but as someone who is no longer pretending not to know.

The torch does not blind. It does not point. It simply makes the next step visible.

The way is shown simply by walking it.
Each moment becomes practice without being named as such.

And then, perhaps, you notice something else.

The cosmic joke.
The eternal struggle.
The Divine play, Lila.

All that seeking.
All that effort.
All that striving to arrive somewhere else.

The endless strategies. The practices. The questions were asked and then asked again. The endless dramas you took on.

Only to discover there was never a departure.

The punchline lands softly.

There is no embarrassment in it. No regret. Just warmth.

You laugh not out loud, but inwardly. A gentle recognition that the seriousness was part of the play. That the forgetting was necessary for the remembering to mean anything at all.

Even illusion served truth.

Without forgetting, there could be no return. No Humor.

Nothing was wrong.

Nothing needed fixing.

You were always what you were looking for.

The path was never hidden.

Only overlooked.

And now, having remembered, you return, not to announce enlightenment, and not to claim understanding, but to live quietly, clearly, and fully, as one more light along the way, helping others see when they stumble into their own darkness.

This is how awakening survives the world.

No banners.
No titles.
No conclusion.

The play continues.

And this time, you know what mask you're wearing.
The stage is still here.
The roles are still played.
The script still unfolds.

But now, the actor knows the difference between costume and self.

And that knowing does not demand any attention.

It simply remains, quietly,  behind every gesture, every word, every breath.

The play continues.

Not because you must perform,

But because you can.



The one who returned
was never away.

The torch illuminates nothing new.

What sees by its light?
