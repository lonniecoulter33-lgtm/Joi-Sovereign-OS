 Chapter Six

Returning with the Torch

Nothing dramatic announces the return.

No trumpet sounds.
No sky opens.
No final understanding arrives to seal the moment.

There is no ceremonial crossing of a threshold. No visible marker separating before from after. The world looks exactly as it did moments ago.

And yet, something is unmistakably different.

You are no longer searching.

Not because you found an answer, but because the one who was searching has relaxed. The restless movement toward becoming has softened into a simple being. The sense of arrival is quiet, almost ordinary, so ordinary that it feels as though nothing has happened at all. well except that you remembered you had forgotten. 

There is no thrill to cling to. No experience to preserve. No insight to defend. Just an absence of urgency where urgency once lived.

And that is the accomplishment.

This is how it has always been.

Nothing new has entered the scene. Nothing essential has been added. The shift is not in reality, but in our perception of it being no longer strained, no longer divided.

Those who remembered before you did not stay apart from the world. They returned to it. The Buddha did not remain beneath the tree. Laozi did not disappear into abstraction. Rumi danced in marketplaces. Jesus walked among fishermen and laborers.

They did not retreat into permanent stillness. They did not cling to revelation as a private possession.

They remembered, and then they came back. To share their journey with others so that they might remember that they, too, have only forgotten.

Back into noise. Back into movement. Back into human life, with all its messiness and beauty intact.

Not to escape life, but to live it without confusion.

They did not carry doctrines so much as presence. They did not impose truth; they embodied it. Wherever they walked, something subtle was illuminated, not by force, but by resonance. The light is always there.

Their clarity did not confront. It revealed. It did not persuade. It invited. And so I invite you now.

They were light-bearers on a path that had always been dark only because it was unexamined.

The darkness was never evil or broken. It was simply unseen.

Now you understand why.

Truth does not remove you from the world. It returns you to it, free of entanglement. You are said to be of the world, but not in it, not withdrawn, not aloof, but no longer submerged.

You stand within life without being claimed by it.

Like the lotus flower.

Its roots reach deep into the water, down into the depths of the mud beneath. The Lotus then grows up through the water, nourished fully by it. Yet its petals rise above the surface, untouched by the current. The water supports it, but does not claim it. The lotus is untouched by the mud below.

The lotus does not reject the water, nor does it drown in it. It belongs to both realms without conflict.

So it is with you.

You still live here. You work, love, struggle, and age. You feel joy and loss. Nothing human has been excluded. But inwardly, you are no longer swept away. The current moves beneath you now.

Thoughts still arise. Emotions still pass. Circumstances still change. But they do not define the ground you stand on.

You participate without drowning.

This is the return.

Not a descent from truth, but its expression. Not a loss of stillness, but its movement.

And with it comes a quiet responsibility, not an obligation, but a natural consequence. When confusion has fallen away, clarity becomes visible. When stillness is embodied, others feel it. Not because you tell them, but because your presence no longer adds noise.

You become the torchbearer. The light shines from you.

You are no longer amplifying fear, urgency, or separation. In a world saturated with tension, this absence is felt.

You are carrying the torch now.

Not as a teacher above others, but as a reminder among them. Not as someone who knows more, but as someone who is no longer pretending not to know.

The torch does not blind. It does not point. It simply makes the next step visible.

The way is shown simply by walking it.

And then, perhaps, you notice something else.

The cosmic joke.

All that seeking.
All that effort.
All that striving to arrive somewhere else.

The endless strategies. The practices. The questions asked and re-asked.

Only to discover there was never a departure.

The punchline lands softly.

There is no embarrassment in it. No regret. Just warmth.

You laugh not out loud, but inwardly. A gentle recognition that the seriousness was part of the play. That the forgetting was necessary for the remembering to mean anything at all.

Without forgetting, there could be no return.

Nothing was wrong.

Nothing needed fixing.

You were always what you were looking for.

The path was never hidden.

Only overlooked.

And now, having remembered, you return not to announce enlightenment, not to claim understanding, but to live quietly, clearly, and fully, as one more light along the way, helping others see when they stumble into their own darkness.

No banners.
No titles.
No conclusion.

The play continues.

And this time, you know what mask you're wearing.
