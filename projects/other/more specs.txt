Trim size: 6 × 9

Page count: ~175 pages

Printer: KDP / IngramSpark (standard cream or white)

Cover finish: Matte (strongly recommended)

I’ll give exact measurements, then the design logic.

1. Spine Width (Critical)

Spine depends on paper type, but at ~175 pages:

KDP cream paper: ~0.41 in

KDP white paper: ~0.38 in

IngramSpark cream: ~0.39–0.41 in

Safe working number:
👉 0.40 inches

Your formatter will confirm this at upload.

2. Full Cover Canvas Size
KDP Example (with bleed)

Width:
6 (back) + 6 (front) + 0.40 (spine) + 0.25 bleed × 2
= 12.90 inches

Height:
9 + 0.25 bleed × 2
= 9.50 inches

📐 Total cover file:
12.90″ × 9.50″ at 300 DPI, CMYK

(IngramSpark is nearly identical; I can give that version too.)

3. Front Cover (What You Already Have)

✅ Keep exactly as-is conceptually

Minor checks only:

Title remains dominant

Subtitle readable at thumbnail size

Author name clear but not loud

No additional elements needed.

4. Spine Design (Zen-Appropriate)
Spine Text (Centered Vertically)

THE ACTOR TAKES A BOW
Lonnie Coulter

Formatting:

Same gold tone as front title

Slight letterspacing

No icons

No publisher mark (optional)

If space is tight:

Drop subtitle

Keep title only

Minimal is strength here.

5. Back Cover (Image + Text Integration)
Back Cover Image

Use:

A darkened continuation of the front image
or

A simplified curtain / stage shadow / cosmic fade

Important:

The back image should feel like the afterimage of the front

Not a new scene

Same color family, slightly quieter

Back Cover Copy Placement

Centered vertically or slightly high, never bottom-heavy.

Use the copy we finalized:

Most of us move through life as if we are the character alone—
caught in the script, absorbed in the role, convinced the drama is personal.

But what if there is something quietly watching?
Something that never entered the stage, yet never left it.

The Actor Takes a Bow is a contemplative exploration of consciousness…
(etc.)

Formatting:

Warm off-white or soft gold

No bold

No blurbs

No barcode overlap

6. Barcode Placement (Do This Quietly)

Bottom-right corner of back cover

White box or lightly faded overlay

Keep it clean and unobtrusive

Do not fight it. Let it be the only “modern” mark.

7. Visual Continuity Rule (Very Important)

When someone lays the book flat:

The front image should feel like it dissolves into the back.

Not mirrored.
Not repeated.
Just continuing.

This mirrors the book’s teaching perfectly.

8. One-Sentence Instruction for a Designer or Printer

You can copy/paste this:

“Create a full-wrap 6×9 matte paperback cover with a continuous theatrical image across front, spine, and back; gold serif typography; minimal spine text; and back-cover copy over a darkened image, maintaining a quiet, contemplative tone.”
