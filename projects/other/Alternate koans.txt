Koan I — Alternate A

(Stillness, origin, breath)

Before the first word
there was a pause.

Before the pause
there was seeing.

Nothing arrived.
Nothing began.

Who noticed?

Koan I — Alternate B

(Theatrical, direct address)

The lights were already on
when you opened your eyes.

No rehearsal.
No entrance cue.

Who has been watching
since before the curtain rose?

Koan II — Alternate A

(Zen mirror)

The mask falls.

The face remains.

The one who sees both
does not move.

Where is the actor now?

Koan II — Alternate B

(Stage imagery)

The costume speaks.

The actor listens.

The stage waits.

Which one are you
when the line is forgotten?

Koan III — Alternate A

(Flow, Tao)

The river does not ask
how far it must go.

The leaf stops floating
when it stops calling itself a leaf.

Where are you being carried?

Koan III — Alternate B

(Minimalist, Zen cut)

Step.

Current.

No footsteps remain.

Who crossed?

Koan IV — Alternate A

(Sensation, disappearance)

A sound enters the room
and leaves without permission.

It never belonged to you.

What heard it vanish?

Koan IV — Alternate B

(Stage / attention)

The spotlight moves.

The object disappears.

The seeing remains
without effort.

Who follows the light?

Koan V — Alternate A

(Silence, transmission)

The mind speaks.

The channel closes.

The mind rests.

What was waiting?

Koan V — Alternate B

(Walkie-talkie metaphor refined)

The button is released.

Nothing is sent.

Everything is received.

Who stopped pressing?

Koan VI — Alternate A

(Return, embodiment)

The traveler arrives
and removes no shoes.

Nothing looks different.

Who returned?

Koan VI — Alternate B

(Torch imagery)

The flame passes.

No hands touch.

The room brightens.

What carried the light?

Koan VII — Alternate A

(Closure without ending)

The bow is taken.

Applause does not arise.

The stage remains.

Who is finished?

Koan VII — Alternate B

(Intermission laughter)

The joke lands.

No one laughs.

The room understands.

Who got the punchline?
