About the Author
Lonnie Coulter is a contemplative writer exploring identity, awareness, and lived presence through direct inquiry. Lonnie Coulter’s journey into awakening began at 17 when a preacher named Richard Little from a local Baptist church handed him a copy of Will Durant’s The Story of Philosophy, which introduced him to Immanuel Kant and Transcendentalism. One solstice night, four years later, while studying Kant’s Critique of Pure Reason, he found himself falling into a deep meditative awareness and witnessed the dissolution of his physical senses while resting in a state of perfect stillness.

After a period of Lucidity while remaining fully awake during R.E.M, he accidentally stumbled into a profound experience of self-awakening that reshaped his understanding of life and consciousness. Since then, Lonnie has immersed himself in Eastern esoteric traditions and Western theosophical research, seeking the language to share these transformative experiences with others. He is a member of the Theosophical Society, a Buddhist teacher, an active Bodhisattva, and a devoted student of Vedanta and Hindu philosophy.

His background as an audio engineer in the vibrant San Francisco music scene gave him a unique perspective on creativity and consciousness. During that time, he collaborated with great minds and philanthropists, including the legendary Merry Pranksters, in efforts to raise awareness and expand human potential within our communities. Lonnie, now 44, is the proud father of two children, Adam Kenneth Coulter and Melody Rose Coulter. He believes that through music, art, and literature, we can elevate consciousness on a societal level and create a better world for future generations. The Actor Takes a Bow is his first book, sharing his vision, an invitation to live consciously in life’s grand theater.
