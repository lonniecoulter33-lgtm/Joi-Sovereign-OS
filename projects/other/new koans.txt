Koan I — Opening Threshold (lightened)

You did not open this book.
The book found you first.

Before the words appeared,
something was already listening.

Who is pretending to read?

Koan II — Identity (warmer, playful)

Memory went looking for its owner.

It checked every name.
None answered.

Remembering shrugged
and kept going.

Koan III — The Mask (gentle irony)

The mask asked,
“If I fall away, what remains?”

The face laughed.

The stage kept its secret.

Koan IV — The Flow (soft wit)

The river has no plan.
The leaf has no résumé.

Still, they arrive on time.

Who taught you to try so hard?

Koan V — Stillness (dry humor)

The button was released.

Nothing dramatic occurred.

Everything worked better.

Who finally stopped helping?

Koan VI — The Return (torch-bearing, warm)

Silence did not keep you.

It handed you a light.

You returned to the stage
without announcing anything.

Who noticed the room grow brighter?


Koan VII — The Completion

The stage vanished.
The audience vanished.

Only seeing remained.

Nothing was inside the play.
Nothing was outside it.

Satisfied,
existence took a breath—

The universe bowed to itself
and began again.
