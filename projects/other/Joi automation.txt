Connector Framework Specification: Joi’s Interface to the World
With your infrastructure locked and your Capability Packs defined, we now establish the Connector Framework. This is the specialized software layer that allows Joi to actually "reach out" and control your grow room, home automation, or studio software.

1. The Anatomy of a Connector
A Connector is a modular piece of code that translates Joi’s high-level intent into the specific technical language (protocol) of a device or app.

Component	Function
The Listener	
Constantly monitors local sensors or app state (e.g., "Is the soil dry?" or "Is Pro Tools recording?").   

The Actuator	
Executes the physical or digital action (e.g., "Turn on the water" or "Switch OBS scene").   

The Authenticator	
Securely uses the keys stored in your Modal Secrets to log into the service.   

2. Interaction Protocols (How she talks to things)
Joi will use different "languages" depending on the target system:

API-Based (Cloud/Web): Used for services like OpenAI, ElevenLabs, or Microsoft Office.   

Local Network (LAN): Used for Home Assistant or MQTT (Grow Room/Home Automation). Joi talks directly to your router to find these devices.

WebSockets: Used for real-time, low-latency control of OBS Studio or Avatar motion.   

OS-Level (Local): Used for apps without APIs (like some parts of Pro Tools), where Joi simulates mouse clicks or keystrokes.

Every connector must support a "Rollback" state. If Joi activates a light or a pump and the sensor doesn't report a change within a set time, she must:

Log the failure in the joi-superai bucket.   

Notify you via the Sidebar "Diagnostics" tab.

🟢 Active: Joi is successfully receiving data from the grow room or studio.
